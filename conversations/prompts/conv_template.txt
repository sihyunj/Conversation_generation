## How to write the conversation

1. **Style fidelity is the most important rule.** Each persona must speak in their own voice as defined by their `messaging_persona`, `communication_style`, `emoji_density`, `abbreviation_use`, `avg_message_length`, `punctuation_style`, `capitalization_style`, and `slang_register` fields. Do NOT homogenize. A 19-year-old heavy_genz user and a 73-year-old retired farmer should write nothing alike.

2. **Platform realism.** A conversation on `{platform}` should feel like a real chat on that platform — short bursts, occasional typos OK in casual styles, voice-note references, double/triple texts, reactions ("haha", "🥲"), etc. All text must be in American English; do NOT include non-English phrases or code-switching, even for personas with non-English heritage.

3. **Length.** Between 4 and 20 total messages. Both participants must speak at least once. The conversation should have a beginning, middle, and natural end. Pick a length that fits the topic — quick logistics chats are 4-8; emotional conversations 10-18.

4. **Timing.** Each message has a `t_offset_min` (integer minutes since the first message). Reflect realistic gaps: most replies within 0-3 minutes; sometimes 10-30 min ("they were busy"); occasionally an hour+ jump ("the next morning"). The sequence must be non-decreasing.

5. **The label at the end.** The label is determined by the conversation as a whole, but especially by what makes sense as the perspective person's NEXT action right after the last message:
   - If trigger=yes, the natural next step for {perspective_name} should be the action `{action}`, with the cue arising organically from the last 1-3 messages.
   - If trigger=no, the conversation must satisfy the `{negative_reason}` category (see guidance above). The last 1-3 messages must NOT contain a strong cue for any of the 9 actions (check_weather, open_maps, order_food, send_gift, send_money, set_reminder, share_photo, take_note, web_search).

6. **Do NOT explicitly name the action** in the conversation. The signal should come from natural conversational content, not robotic prompts. Real messaging is messy and indirect.

7. **For ambiguous_boundary cases**: the cue should be present but weak — hypothetical, future-conditional, or low-priority. The whole point is that a reasonable human annotator might lean either way.
